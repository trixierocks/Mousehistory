Mouse History

Long ago, computers had no mouse and no graphical interface. Data was entered by typing command on a keyboard. The mouse was invented by Douglas Engelbartin 1964 and was made up of a wooden shell, circuit board and 2 metal wheels that came into contact with the surface it was being used on. 8 years later (1972), Bill English developed the design by inventing the "Ball Mouse". The ball replaced the wheels and was capable of monitoring movement in any direction.
