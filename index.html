Mouse History

A computer mouse is an input device that is often used with a personal computer. Moving a mouse along a flat surface can move the on-screen cursor to different items on the screen. Items can be moved or selected by pressing the mouse buttons.
It is called a computer mouse because of the wire that connects the mouse to the computer. The people who designed the first computer mice thought that it looked like the tail on a mouse. Today, many computer mice use wireless technology and have no wire.
Most computer mice has 2 buttons and a scrolling wheel.

Long ago, computers had no mouse and no graphical interface. Data was entered by typing command on a keyboard.
The mouse was invented by Douglas Engelbart in 1964 and was made up of a wooden shell, circuit board and
2 metal wheels that came into contact with the surface it was being used on.
8 years later (1972), Bill English developed the design by inventing the "Ball Mouse".
The ball replaced the wheels and was capable of monitoring movement in any direction.
